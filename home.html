Introduction

Heart disease, kidney disease, and cancer are among the most prevalent and life-threatening health conditions, affecting millions of individuals worldwide each year. Despite significant advancements in medical treatments, it remains essential to understand the root causes of these diseases. Researchers continue to examine the intricate relationship between biological and genetic factors (such as gender, race, and age) and lifestyle behaviors (including physical activity, smoking, and alcohol consumption) in order to better predict, prevent, and manage these conditions.

For our final project, we chose to explore a healthcare dataset that highlights key indicators associated with heart disease. This dataset, titled "Indicators of Heart Disease", was developed by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) as part of the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS)—an ongoing nationwide survey that collects data on health-related behaviors and conditions from U.S. residents through annual telephone interviews. The dataset is publicly available on Kaggle: Indicators of Heart Disease (2022 UPDATE) . It contains 319,795 observations and 18 features, including: HeartDisease, BMI, Smoking, AlcoholDrinking, Stroke, PhysicalHealth, MentalHealth, DiffWalking, Sex, AgeCategory, Race, Diabetic, PhysicalActivity, GenHealth, SleepTime, Asthma, KidneyDisease, and SkinCancer.

This website features a series of interactive data visualizations and research questions that examine the risk factors associated with heart disease, kidney disease, and skin cancer. You can explore the research questions here and view the visualizations here.
